Slide 2 — The tension

Add an agentic layer — without breaking how anyone works today.

  • Users keep working exactly as they do now
  • Automation earns its way in, suggestion by suggestion
  • Trust is the currency — lose it once, adoption stalls

Notes: The core constraint from the vision doc. Everything in the demo is shaped by it: nothing acts without the user's approval, and ignoring Eva entirely costs the user nothing.

Slide 3 — Three new jobs emerge

When automation works on the accountant's behalf:

  1. Review & Triage — "What can I trust — and what needs my eyes?"
  2. Configure & Maintain — "Which patterns are worth automating?"
  3. Trust & Accountability — "Can I explain decisions I didn't make myself?"

The role shifts: Do → Review → Operate → Supervise

Notes: One sentence per job, no more. Tell them every moment of the demo answers one of these three — ask them to keep score.

Slide 5 — What you just saw

  • One suggestion language — value · confidence % · reason · time saved, same at every scale
  • A ladder — cell suggestions become workflows once the pattern holds
  • One drawer geometry — Eva lives in a left drawer, artifacts open to its right; all other drawers open from page context, anchored right
  • Artifacts as Eva's working surface — work products assembled beside the page: reviewable, actionable, reusable
  • The log as the source of truth — who, what and when, and the history of what automation has done

Notes: This slide turns the demo into vocabulary. Pause here — first natural question moment.

Slide 6 — Five commitments the design makes (1/2)

  1. One suggestion language, always fronted by Eva — whatever engine produced it
  2. Automation lives in one layer, not one place — surfaces in context, always resolves in the Workflow drawer
  3. Every automation, no matter where it was born, becomes the same thing: a workflow — with act vs. suggest as a first-class parameter

Notes: On #3, spell out act-vs-suggest: "Bogfør på konto 7220" acts; "Foreslå fuld tekst" suggests. One engine spans deterministic and agentic — and it gives users a trust dial: start in suggest mode, promote to act mode once proven.

Slide 7 — Five commitments the design makes (2/2)

  1. We're working towards artifacts — Eva's work happens in artifacts beside the page, never by mutating the page; changes land via accepted suggestions and activated workflows
  2. The log is the source of truth — who, what and when; the history of what automation has done

Challenge these now — everything after assumes them.

Notes: Frame #4 as a direction, not a guardrail: artifacts are the working surface we're building towards. As automation matures, users work increasingly page-agnostic — artifacts are where Eva-assembled work happens, and pages remain the system of record. Invite objections explicitly. If a commitment is seriously contested, park it with a name attached and move on — don't resolve it live.

Slide 8 — Who owns what

  • Eva team — the suggestion language: anatomy, confidence display, reasoning, identity, promotion
  • Workflows team — the engine: triggers, actions, runs, errors, dashboard
  • The shared contract: the workflow suggestion card — Eva produces it, Workflows runs it. Jointly owned, versioned.

Notes: Rationale for Eva as main owner: the Eva mark fronts every suggestion, so users hold Eva accountable for all of it — brand and responsibility need one owner.

Slide 9 — Decide today (switch to the Slite checklist)

Seven contracts — cheap to set now, expensive to retrofit:

  1. Suggestion anatomy — cell, entry, page, workflow
  2. Suggestion → workflow promotion contract (the card as API)
  3. Every automation becomes the same thing: a workflow (act vs. suggest parameter)
  4. Log event contract — the source of truth (who · what · when); every mutation logs
  5. Artifacts as Eva's working surface — no direct page mutation
  6. Ownership split
  7. Drawer geometry — Eva in a left drawer with artifacts to its right; all other drawers open from page context, anchored right

Per item: any objections? Who owns it?

Notes: Work this from the Slite analysis doc so you can tick/assign live. ~3 minutes per item. Outcome per item: ticked, assigned, or parked with a name. Don't debate solutions in the room.
